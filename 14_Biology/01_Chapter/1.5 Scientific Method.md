**Scientific method**
---
1. **Observations** 
2. **Questions** - generate questions based on the observations
3. **Hypothesis** - proposing answers and explanations for those questions. 
4. **Predictions** - determine what would happen based on those hypotheses 
5. **Testing** - Conduct an experiment to see if the predictions hold. 

**Types of Biological Experiments**
---
- **Controlled Experiments** - single variable or single factor in an experimental group and compare it with a non-manipulated control group
	  
- **Comparative Experiments** - two differences between two different groups. 


**Data and Statistics**
---

**Statistical Significance** - it determines if the difference between each group are base of truly meaningful or just due to chance

**Null Hypothesis** - assumption of the statistical. and have the idea that no difference exists between the groups being compared.
